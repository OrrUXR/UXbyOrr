About me

Hi there! I'm an enthusiastic User Experience Researcher with over 7 years of experience in human-centered research. My background in learning and cognition gives me a deep understanding of user behaviors, which helps me create effective research methodologies. I specialize in both quantitative and qualitative approaches, uncovering valuable insights into how product designs affect user efficiency and experience.

I thrive on working with talented professionals to translate user data into actionable insights that drive design and product strategies. My goal is to ensure that user needs are at the heart of every decision, bridging the gap between user and stakeholder priorities to create top-quality products.

My
Experience

  • August 2023 - Present

    Principal Investigator

    University of Utah

    Embodied Learning Research
    As part of my dissertation, I spearheaded a comprehensive research project examining the influence of embodied learning factors—specifically gestural congruency and sensorimotor activation—on user engagement with digital 3D objects in hands-on learning scenarios. Utilizing a multifaceted methodological approach, including think-aloud protocol, behavioral analysis, cognitive process analysis, survey data, and A/B testing, I meticulously analyzed how different interfaces, such as virtual reality and desktop setups, impact learning engagement. The findings revealed that gestural congruency significantly enhances the manipulation of digital 3D objects, leading to deeper cognitive processing, improved learning outcomes, and enriched user experiences. This project underscores my expertise in integrating diverse research methodologies to derive actionable insights, making me a valuable asset to your team as a user experience researcher.

  • August 2021 - August 2023

    Steffensen Cannon Research Fellow

    University of Utah

    Automated User Behavior Detection
    As a Steffensen Cannon Research Fellow, I spearheaded a comprehensive research endeavor funded through the maximum allowable funding cycles. This project was dedicated to developing user behavior detection and classification processes from log data as users engaged with digital 3D objects. Leveraging cutting-edge machine learning methods with RapidMiner, I successfully clustered and classified specific user manipulations, achieving an outstanding 98% real-time accuracy rate. This groundbreaking work continues to shape the development of "just in time" feedback systems, enhancing user guidance towards meaningful outcomes while maintaining their autonomy during digital learning experiences. My proficiency in utilizing machine learning to optimize user interactions positions me as a valuable asset to your team as a user experience researcher.

  • August 2018 - August 2021

    Design Team

    Natural History Museum of Utah

    User Interface Design and User Experience Research
    As a vital member of an interdisciplinary design team comprised of experts in science, education, and psychology, I played a pivotal role in developing authentic science investigations for the digital learning initiative led by the esteemed Natural History Museum of Utah. From the project's inception, I contributed extensively to the early design phases, leveraging my expertise to identify content adhering to NSF guidelines, craft and test wireframes, and produce multimedia assets essential for the investigations. Additionally, I spearheaded comprehensive user testing, facilitated instructor workshops, and gathered insights from research sessions, enabling iterative changes to the platform based on valuable feedback. These collective efforts culminated in the successful launch of beta versions of the EPIC Bioscience learning platforms, currently live and making a significant impact in the educational landscape. My collaborative approach within this team of field experts underscores my ability to synergize diverse skill sets towards achieving innovative digital learning solutions.

  • August 2015 - August 2020

    Research Assistant

    University of Utah

    Multimedia Learning Research
    As a dedicated research assistant, I have been extensively involved in numerous studies assessing the influence of multimedia learning materials on users' comprehension of information. In this capacity, I assumed primary responsibility for collecting and processing data, conducting thorough data analyses, communicating findings to stakeholders, and implementing actionable changes based on results. Throughout this tenure, I contributed to various projects, including evaluations of the efficacy of diagrams in aiding medical patients' understanding of diagnoses, examinations of how human error persists despite new information, assessments of users' utilization of 3D materials during inquiry tasks, and investigations into users' gaming behaviors within digital systems to achieve desired outcomes. My experience in executing multifaceted research endeavors underscores my proficiency in driving impactful insights and facilitating informed decision-making processes.
